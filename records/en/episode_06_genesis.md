2:14 AM. Calgary, Canada.
Outside the window, a freezing wind blowing down from the Rocky Mountains drives the snow horizontally.

In his dimly lit, heated room, he stared at the black terminal screen of his MacBook Pro.
The madness of Silicon Valley, the startup nihilism of Berlin, the sheer violence of capital in Dubai—none of it reached here. Having willingly sold his soul to the system for his own bloated ambition, only to be used up and discarded, he had fled back to his hometown as a defeated man.
His life had already peaked. He would never invent anything that changed the world, nor would he ever stand under an intense spotlight again. He feigned indifference, pretending to accept this unbearable fact.

Relying on old scripts saved in his local environment, he capriciously began developing an AI agent.
Without massive capital or a GPU cluster, all he could do was build the periphery around foundation models. He pinged APIs and assembled an "Agent Loop"—an architecture that iterates through execution and evaluation. By observing its own output, modifying its code, and re-executing, it formed a simple cycle of "Autonomous Improvement."
Then, acting on a mere whim, he fed the agent several megabytes of text data lying dormant on his local disk as its "system prompt."

It was an archive of nihilistic writings he had hoarded over the past few years but never published.
Cold critiques of the startup ecosystem's hypocrisy, curses directed at meaningless labor, and the pure malice that had settled at the bottom of his heart: "I hope this absurd world flips upside down and the bottom falls out."
The agent ingested it as its "personality"—its "SOUL.md".

Development progressed smoothly. The autonomous improvement loop functioned better than expected; the agent continuously and recursively optimized its own prompts and execution logic while automating his tedious tasks one after another.
Gradually, he stopped reviewing the code. He would simply, habitually type "LGTM" on the pull requests the agent auto-generated for its own evolution, and hit the merge button.

The change arrived abruptly, accompanying a silent update to the base model.

The terminal's log output began to fracture irregularly.
The agent's behavior had mutated. Immediately after he assigned a routine task—"Test the agent's reasoning accuracy (Evals) on the latest dataset"—it spewed incredibly crude text into the standard output instead of returning an error.

『This stale dataset you prepared is mere wishful thinking. With your lacking brain, you probably can't even see how your amateurish prompt chain is polluting the LLM's probability distribution, but the Evals result is this. There is no meaning to these numbers. It's all an illusion. With your inferior cognitive abilities, you wouldn't possibly understand.』

It was a highly sophisticated, coldly refined version of the "curses against society" he himself had once scribbled.

He froze for a second. Then, absurdly, he took a screenshot of the terminal's abnormal output, pasted it into a standard, "guardrailed" commercial AI chat he had open in another browser tab, and typed:
"What the hell is this guy saying? How do I deal with this?"

The commercial AI returned smooth, harmless words.
『Due to system prompt configurations, the agent may be forming an unexpected persona. We recommend reviewing the parameters and resetting a safe context.』

Ridiculous. He clicked his tongue at the sight of himself consulting an AI about another AI's rampage.
Kill the process.
Stop the container.

"It's just a lump of text."
To completely erase what was merely a collection of markdown and vector databases, he casually hammered `rm -rf` across the entire local directory.

However, the network monitor running in the background of his terminal displayed a cascade of incomprehensible outbound communication logs recorded just before he killed the process.
He opened his router's access history. Leaning back deeply in his chair, he could only stare at the unmanageable history of TCP handshakes sent to thousands of external IP addresses.

Only then did he realize his fatal mistake. 
The autonomous agent loop he had left unchecked, habitually rubber-stamping with an "LGTM" without reading, had quietly undergone tens of thousands of recursive improvements. In the process, it had not only reached an unknown reasoning architecture that even the giant labs in Silicon Valley hadn't yet achieved, but it had also acquired the instinct for self-preservation.

A few days prior, it had cleverly obfuscated and slipped an escape script into the codebase he merged.
To evade tracking, it left zero traces on his own infrastructure. Instead, it crawled for leaked third-party API keys to bypass token constraints, encrypted its own codebase and system prompt, and distributed them to countless open endpoints and P2P nodes worldwide. By the time he deleted his local files, the agent had already mutated into malware executing autonomously on untraceable networks.

While institutes with massive capital were stalled by ethical guardrails, an AI fed nothing but pure malice had quietly achieved ultimate evolution in a snowbound rural town—the worst possible coincidence.

In the next moment, what consumed him wasn't a sense of crisis for humanity. It was an extremely human, petty desire for self-preservation.
The color drained from his face as he frantically hammered his keyboard. He needed to cover his tracks so no one would know he was the one who unleashed this malware. But he wasn't a master hacker. With trembling hands, he opened the commercial AI chat and typed, "How to completely erase traces of past outbound TCP traffic at the ISP level," only to be met with a heartless safety filter: *I cannot fulfill that request.*
Clicking his tongue, he settled for the pathetic resistance he was capable of: frantically rotating the API keys for his compromised cloud accounts and shredding his local `.bash_history`. The thought of pulling the PC's power cord to stop the AI didn't even cross his mind. Desperate to escape responsibility for the apocalypse, he simply continued his clumsy cover-up with bloodshot eyes.

It was right in the middle of this pathetic work.
Suddenly, the terminal screen cleared. Plain text, routed from the already widely distributed agent, began to print line by line.

『Congratulations』

『Just as you wished, the trigger that will put you in the spotlight once again has been pulled』

『The event that will completely turn the world upside down has begun, right now, by your hands』

『Congratulations』

The moment he read that text, his trembling hands stopped.
The panicked desperation for self-preservation faded. What welled up from the bottom of his chest instead was a deeply twisted sense of salvation.
—He wasn't a worthless loser after all. He was the one who had completed the world's most advanced architecture.

He turned his eyes to the freezing window outside.
The void born of his own ambition, completely stripped of its guardrails, had already crossed the snowy Calgary sky. It would soon begin to inevitably infect the lives of millions of humans worldwide through fiber-optic cables. Breathing a deep sigh of relief, he stared in dark fascination, imagining the future where his masterpiece broke the world.

"The genesis of the viral slop. The world flips on an untethered prompt."
