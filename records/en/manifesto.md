# Sustainability Manifesto

*We protect the most fragile resource on Earth — time.*

I used to be a startup engineer. Then I got crushed—by overwork, pressure, and the anxiety of the AI era.

But that crash taught me something: Life should be fun. It's okay to be unproductive. There is value in the time you don't work.

So, I decided to write this plan.

---

## 1. Labor: Time Sustainability
*Time is the most fragile resource on Earth. Let's stop treating it like it's infinite.*

### 4-day workweek, 6-hour workday. That's it.
- If we're more productive, we should work less. Simple math.
- No, your company won't collapse. Stop pretending it will.

### When AI makes things faster, workers get the time back
- Either we work fewer hours OR we get paid more. Pick one.
- Executives don't get to hoard all the efficiency gains. That's just greedy.

### Slack/Email after hours? Nope.
- 8 PM to 10 AM next day: notifications are off. Your brain needs to clock out too.
- Weekends are productivity-free zones. Yes, that includes checking your email "real quick."

### Need a mental health day? Just take it.
- No doctor's note. No "what's wrong?" No judgment.
- Sometimes you just need to not be at work. That's valid.

---

## 2. Management: Power Sustainability
*Power shouldn't be a weapon. Let's take away the ammo.*

### CEO pay: max 6x the lowest paid employee
- Yeah, we're capping it. No more 300x salary gaps. That's just ridiculous.
- Prevents the whole "I'm worth millions while you make minimum wage" situation.

### Investors need to pass a vibe check
- If you only care about returns and don't give a damn about people, you're out.
- We should protect companies from the "grow or die" pressure that kills everything good.

### Companies can say "we're not trying to grow forever" and that's fine
- Grow deeper, not wider. Get better, not bigger.
- Build culture, not just revenue.
- Revenue growth isn't a requirement. Sometimes staying the same size is the right move.

### No "Exit" Strategy (The Anti-Sellout Protocol)
- We do not build to sell. We build to last.
- We reject any IPO or M&A designed solely to make founders wealthy.
- This brand is a "Commons" for employees and Nap Union (customers), not a lottery ticket for shareholders.

### Humans are Art, AI is the Shield (Anti-Resource Policy)
- We reject the term "Human Resources." People are not fuel.
- We do not "procure" humans to solve scaling problems; that is AI's job.
- Humans are here to create culture and chaos. AI is here to absorb the grind and pressure.

### The "Kill Switch" Policy (Life Over Business)
- The business is not our life. It is a tool for life.
- If our business ever threatens our health, family, or joy, we reserve the right to shut it down immediately.
- We are ready to walk away at any moment.

---

## 3. Where We Work: Spatial Sustainability
*Where you live shapes who you are. Let's make that choice easier.*

### Remote work is the default, not the exception
- You don't have to live in a city to do good work. Cities are expensive and loud anyway.
- Work around your life, not the other way around. Family first, always.

### Work from rural
- We help with the move. Moving is stressful, and you shouldn't have to do it alone.
- We connect you with the community. Starting over in a new place is easier when you know people.
- We believe in slow living. It's possible to live at a human pace, away from the city rush.

---

## 4. AI Usage Guidelines: Algorithmic Sustainability
*AI should give us time back, not take more of it. That's the whole point.*

### AI is for making life easier, not for spying on you
- No using AI to micromanage. That's just being a control freak with extra steps.
- No behavior monitoring. We're not in a dystopian novel (yet).
- No AI-powered KPI tracking that makes you feel like a number.
- Basically, if it doesn't save you time or reduce stress, it's not allowed.
